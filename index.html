About me

I am currently a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Technology at Westlake University, focusing on bionic robot motion planning. My research explores the intersection of robotics, deep reinforcement learning, and hydrodynamics to design efficient bio-inspired propulsion systems. Starting in April 2026, I will expand my research as a Visiting Scholar at Penn State University.

Beyond academic research and programming in Python, I have maintained a lifelong passion for physical training, badminton, and singing—pursuits I have genuinely enjoyed since childhood. I also had the wonderful experience of performing with the Xinyue Choir for a year. In my free time, I love capturing the world through photography and refining images using Photoshop.
